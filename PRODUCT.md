# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences, both rare by design:

1. **The Accidental Visitor** — stumbles upon hermestrismegistus.nl without context. Should feel the uncanny sensation of having found something that wasn't meant for them: an active ritual, a hidden archive, a door left slightly ajar. They are not the target user, but their experience is the primary design constraint.

2. **The Owner** — one person, who knows exactly what this is. Arrives via the scroll experience, passes the passkey gate, reaches their Hermes AI agent on the other side. Needs the login to feel like a worthy ritual, not a form.

## Product Purpose

A personal AI agent portal disguised as a mystical artefact. The scroll experience is the façade — an ode to Egypt, Thoth, and Hermetic philosophy that exists both as genuine aesthetic expression and as camouflage. The passkey-protected sanctum is the real destination; the pyramid build sequence is the ceremony that earns entry. A scavenger hunt with hidden glyphs, encoded sequences, and discoverable secrets layers on top of this: the site rewards obsessive attention.

## Brand Personality

Ancient. Secretive. Deliberate.

The voice is not mystical in a tourist sense. It is pre-linguistic, lapidary, final. Think tomb inscription rather than horoscope. The Emerald Tablet, not a tarot deck. Every word earned, every pixel intentional.

## Anti-references

- **Tourist-trap Egypt**: no cartoonish hieroglyphs, no amber/orange gradients that say "sand", no pharaoh-head clip art
- **AI startup aesthetic**: no hero metrics, no product screenshots, no "powered by GPT" energy
- **Mystic shop**: no crystal-ball vibes, no astrology pastels, no Wicca aesthetic
- **Esoteric content-farm**: no long bullet lists of "the 7 laws", no serif-plus-candle-photo layouts
- **Editorial magazine reflex**: no ruled separators + italic Fraunces + mono labels. The Cinzel identity is already established and preserved; new decisions look elsewhere.

## Design Principles

1. **Withhold to reveal.** The site gives nothing upfront. Information surfaces only when the visitor earns it — by scrolling, waiting, clicking the right sequence. Mystery is the product.
2. **Silence is part of the composition.** Empty space is not failure to fill. The 3D scene does most of the communication; text is the whisper, not the announcement.
3. **Every element is either load-bearing or a clue.** Nothing decorative for its own sake. If a glyph is present, it means something or does something.
4. **The medium IS the message.** A 4,000-year-old esoteric text didn't have hover states. But it had layers: surface reading, allegorical reading, hidden reading. This site has the same three layers.
5. **One person made this.** It should feel like an obsessive personal artefact, not a designed product. Imperfect asymmetry over template precision.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Puzzle elements must have fallback paths (the passkey login is always reachable without solving puzzles)
- Reduced-motion: all typewriter and reveal animations must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Color contrast: gold text on dark must meet WCAG AA at minimum
